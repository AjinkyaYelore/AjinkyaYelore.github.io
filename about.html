About my profile

I am a dedicated Electrical Research Fellow with a passion for advancing electrical engineering through innovative research and practical applications. Currently, I work as a Research Fellow at the Indian Defence Organisation. I focus on cutting-edge research into aircraft generators and their control unit. Bachelor of Engineering in Electrical Engineering, Rashtrasant Tukadoji Maharaj Nagpur University. My primary research focus is on improving aircraft generator stability, reliability, and efficiency. Furthermore, I am exploring mathematical modelling of generators and validating them with real-time scenarios. I have over one year 10 months of experience in electrical engineering research. Junior Research fellow at Indian Defence Organisation. These experiences have equipped me with valuable insights into real-world challenges and opportunities in the field. I have authored 2 research papers published in renowned international journals and presented my work at major conferences. Some of my contributions include novel control algorithms for aircraft generators, optimization techniques to reduce the weight-to-power ratio, and mathematical modelling of an aircraft generator and its control unit. I have collaborated with industry leaders, scientists, utility companies, and fellow researchers to implement pilot projects for electric drives. These partnerships have enabled me to bridge the gap between research and industry, ensuring my research is both practical and relevant. Proficient in MATLAB, Simulink, Candence OrCAD and Allegro, KiCAD, Proteus, Code Composer Studio, Vim editor for hardware design modelling, coding and simulation, LDRA testing tool, NI LabVIEW. Machine design software: Simcenter motor solver and MAGNET software.

An extensive background in the design of electronic circuits, power electronics, control systems, real-time operating systems, communication protocols, and documentation. I am committed to continuing my research into space electronics solutions, striving to create a positive impact on the global space landscape. My long-term goal is to lead collaborative research projects that promote widespread adoption of space electronics technologies for a greener future. As part of my commitment to knowledge dissemination, I actively mentor graduate students and aspiring researchers. I find joy in nurturing the next generation of electrical engineers and fostering their interest in research and innovation. Outside of work, I enjoy outdoor activities such as badminton and indoor activities such as chess. I also have a keen interest in volunteering on the Skills India volunteer program.
